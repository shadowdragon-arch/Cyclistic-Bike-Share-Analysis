# Cyclistic-Bike-Share-Analysis
# Cyclistic Bike-Share: A Case Study in Data Analytics
### Portfolio Project

---

## 1. Business Task
The objective of this analysis is to identify behavioral differences between annual members and casual riders to help design a marketing strategy aimed at converting casual users into long-term members.

## 2. Data Cleaning & Manipulation
Using **Python (Pandas)**, I processed approximately 5.6 million rows of historical trip data:
* Merged 12 monthly CSV files into a single unified DataFrame.
* Removed null values and duplicate records to ensure data quality.
* Created features for `ride_length` and `day_of_week` to facilitate deeper analysis.

## 3. Key Findings
* **Usage Patterns:** Casual riders peak significantly on **Saturdays**, while members show high, consistent usage on **Tuesdays and Wednesdays**.
* **Seasonality:** Both groups reach peak usage in **August**, but casual riders show a much sharper decline during winter months.
* **Time of Day:** Members exhibit "rush hour" spikes at 8 AM and 5 PM, whereas casual riders peak steadily in the afternoon.

## 4. Marketing Recommendations
1. **Spring Conversion Kick-off:** Launch membership promotions in **April/May** to capture casual riders before the summer peak.
2. **Weekend-Specific Incentives:** Develop a trial membership focused on weekend leisure riders to bridge the gap between casual and annual use.
3. **Price-Transparency Ads:** Use digital ads during peak afternoon hours to show casual riders the long-term cost savings of an annual membership.
